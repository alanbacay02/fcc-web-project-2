Gabe Newell:

Valve and the Path to Success

Portrait of American video game developer Gabe Newell, photographed at Valve Corporations offices in Bellevue, Washington

"The programmers of tommorrow are the wizards of the future"
- Gabe Newell

Gabe Newell is the co-founder and current president of Valve. He led the development of its digital distribution service Steam, the largest PC video game vendor on Earth. Stardock estimated that of 2009, Steam had a 70% share of the digital distribution market for PC video games.

In 2011, Forbes reported that Steam Sales constituted 50-70% of the US $4 billion market for downloaded PC games, controlling most of the market for downloaded PC games.

He is one of the wealthiest people in the United states. Forbes estimated in December 2021 that his net worth is at US$3.9 billion.

Timeline of the life
of Gabe Newell and Valve:

  • 1962 - Gabe Newell was born on November 3rd, 1962 in Seattle, Washington, United States. As he grew older, his interest in solving puzzles intensified and developed into a passion for computer games and information technology.
  • 1980 - Gabe Newell enrolled at Harvard University but was convinced to drop out to work for Bill Gates at Microsoft by the head of sales in 1983.
  • 1983 - Gabe Newell spent 13 years at Microsoft as the lead developer of the first three releases of the Windows operating systems, making him a millionaire. Newell said he learned more during his first three months at Microsoft that he ever did at Harvard.
  • 1995 - Doom, a 1993 first person shooter game developed by id Software, was estimated to be installed on more computers worldwide than Microsoft's new OS, Windows 95. Newell said "Microsoft was a hiring 500-people sales teams and this company (id Software) was 12 people, yet it had created the most widely distributed software in the world. There was a sea change coming."
  • 1996 - Newell and another employee, Mike Harrington, left Microsoft to found the video game company Valve Corporation on August 24, 1996. Newell and Harrington funded the development of the first Valve game Half-Life, which was a critical success. He is one of the pioneers in the field of advanced graphics and AI in gaming.
  • 1999 - Newell gave Valve no deadline and a "virtually unlimited" budget to develop Half-life 2. Meanwhile, he spent several months developing Steam.
  • 2000 - Mike Harrington leaves Valve, leaving Gabe Newell as the sole owner of Valve.
  • Early 2003 - By May of 2003, Valve had already released multiple games, most notable of which are Ricochet, Day of Defeat, and Counter Strike, the most successful first person shoot game of all time. At that time, Counter Strike was plagued by cheating and piracy,and its player base was fragmented across multiple versions.
  • Late 2003 - Valve releases Steam, its video game distribution software, which solved most of the pain points of Valve. Steam allowed all Valve games to be automatically updated for its player base and reduced cheating through Valve Anti-Cheat (VAC). Steam was also unique because of its streamline videogame installation process.
  • 2004 - Half Life 2 was the first game to be offered digitally on Steam and require installation of the Steam client to play for retail copies.
  • 2005 - After Valve recognized the degree of fragmentation between game developers and the high costs of physically publishing and distributing games, Valve opened the Steam platform to allow non-Valve Games to be sold and played through Steam.
  • 2008 - Steam releases the 'New Releases' tab to help consumers sift through the flood of games appearing on the platform. Steam's developer-friendly services made it an attractive place to list games on, starting a golden age for PC game development.
  • 2014 - Steam releases the 'Discovery Update' that would allow consumers to find games based on the recommendations of friends and community curators. Steam saw an 18% increase in all sale revenues while 3 million Steam users had found new games through community curators.
  • 2019 - Steam has over 1 billion registered accounts with 90 million active users and upwards 14 million daily concurrent users - double the amount in 2015. Steam has made $4.3 billion in sale revenues that accounted for 14% of all gaming revenues while showing no signs of slowing down.
  • 2021 - Gabe Newell, now a billionaire, owns at lease on quarter of Valve making him the richest person in the video game industry. During the COVID-19 Pandemic, Newell resided in New Zealand and applied for a permanent residency. He did not however intend to take Valve offices with him and returned to Valve in Seattle in 2021.
  • Today - On Febuary 2022, he launched the Steam Deck, Valves own handheld portable gaming computer that runs on SteamOS, Valve's own Linux-based operating system. Gabe Newell personally hand delivered a few Steam Decks signed by himself. He joked that delivering the Steam Decks was "bringing him back" to his days as a delivery man.

Gabe Newell's release of Steam forever changed the landscape of the video game industry, shaping it to what we know of today. Steam still remains the most widely-used digital storefront online. While he no longer leads the video game development at Valve, he is still at the forefront of its technological development.
